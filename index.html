Art and Computer Science

“I have always imagined that Paradise will be a kind of library”

During my education, one of the most influential classes I took was “Literature in the Age of AI” taught by professor Dennis Tenen. My eyes were opened to the ways that computer science, philosophy, and art could intermix and I wanted to contribute in my own way. My final project for the class explored the universal language developed by philosopher John Wilkins, and sought to recreate it using modern AI tools.

I have always been passionate about the visual arts, and have an array of work you can check out here. In the future, I hope to create pieces that combine art and computer science into something new.
